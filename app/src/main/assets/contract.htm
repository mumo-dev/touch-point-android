TOUCH POINT LICENSE AGREEMENT FOR THE USE OF GUICHET UNIQUE LIMITEDS
MULTIMODAL PLATFORM

 

ON THE OTHER HAND,

 

Hereinafter referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties".

 

 

FOREWORD:

 

1.        WHEREAS, GU Ltd has an IT solution in the form of a multimodal platform enabling its distribution network made of independent operators running diverse hereinafter referred to as "Touch Points" to access and sell various Transactional Services (as defined below) and have a tool for managing such Services (the "Multimodal Platform"). This solution allows, in particular, to:

 

-          aggregate the transactional services used by the partners to allow global visibility on all transactions as well as the use of a single support regardless of the service used;

 

-          have a central system (back-office) ensuring the management of all services provided, and the collection of direct payments made via the one-stop software package of GU Ltd  (hereinafter referred to as the "One-Stop Software Package");

 

-          have access to the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd in order to provide the Services negotiated with Partners.

 

2.        WHEREAS, the company or Mr , as a Touch Point, wishes to use the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd to distribute the Services catalog of the One-Stop Software Package.

 

3.        The Parties thus wished to conclude this Agreement allowing the Touch Point to have access to the Multimodal Platform to offer services including loading and collection of e-money and any other Service under the conditions specified below.

 

 

THE FOLLOWING HAS BEEN AGREED AND DECIDED UPON:

 

Article 1. Definitions

 

Annexes: refers to the documents attached hereto.

 

General Terms of Use (GTU): refers to the terms and conditions of use of the Services enacted by the PARTNERS and applicable to GU Ltd, to the Touch Point and its potential customers, and provided to the Touch Point by GU Ltd during the operational training.

 

INTOUCH Account (ITA): the single account associated with a Touch Point. This account, registered into the GU Ltd system, contains the essential information for the identification of the Touch Point.

 

Connectivity: refers to the Internet connection used to access the One-Stop Software Package application via the Device and provided by GU Ltd.

 

Agreement: refers to this Agreement.

 

Personal data: refers to any information relating to a natural person identified, directly or indirectly, by reference to an identification number or to several specific elements.

 

Device: refers to a mobile digital device used as a medium to provide the services on the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd.

 

Partner: the entity whose Services are offered in the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd.

 

Touch Point: natural or legal person, Total station operator, distributing to customers, as part of an Agreement with GU Ltd, the services of the One-Stop Software Package via the Multimodal Platform.

 

Services: refers to the Partners services sold by the Touch Point via the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd.

 

Promotional materials: materials used by GU Ltd for marketing, promotion and advertising purposes.

 

Transaction(s): refers to the operations related to the Services performed by the Touch Point through the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd including (i) cash deposit for the purchase of e-money, (ii) money transfer, and (iii) collection of bills.

 

 

Article 2. PURPOSE

 

This Agreement defines the relationship between GU Ltd and the Touch Point to ensure the required supply in order to distribute all present and future Services via the Multimodal Platform.

 

Article 3. Role of GU Ltd 

 

As part of its relationship with the Touch Point, GU Ltd will act as the manager of the Multimodal Platform, including through the implementation of integration processes for the distribution of the Partners Services.

 

Article 4 Obligations of THE PARTIES

 

4.1 GU Ltd.

 

GU Ltd is committed to:

 

-          make available to the Touch Point the Multimodal Platform;

-          assist the Touch Point in opening his account on the Multimodal Platform of GU Ltd and ensure that he can perform the Services offered by the Partners;

-          provision the INTOUCH Account (ITA) of the Touch Point in accordance with the deposits made by the latter;

-          provide the Touch Point with a remote support free of charge in the form of a hotline opened from Monday to Sunday, from 7am to 11pm for request processing;

-          follow up, at any time and without prior notice, the commercial operations of the Touch Point to ensure that they comply with the terms of this Agreement and the applicable regulations;

-          decide, at any time, the temporary (for a period not exceeding 30 days) or permanent suspension of the INTOUCH Account of the Touch Point in case of non-compliance with the terms of this Agreement as well as in case of non-compliance with the General Terms of Use (GTU) of the Services deployed on the Multimodal Platform and with any new requirements from the Partners communicated to the Touch Point by GU Ltd;

-          inform the Touch Point within a reasonable timescale of any malfunction of the Multimodal Platform;

-          keep a history and traceability of the Transactions in accordance with the legislation in force in the country where such Transactions are carried out;

-          inform the Touch Point of any regulatory and/or contractual changes relating to his service delivery;

-          immediately inform the Touch Point in the event of fraud, suspicion of fraud and/or irregularity from one of his customers;

-          sell the device on which the One-Stop Software Package application will be previously installed;

 

 

4.2 THE TOUCH POINT

 

Business Activity Management.

The Touch Point is committed to:

 

-          display at his premises in a way visible for customers any promotional materials made available by Total or the Partners and ensure they are in good condition. If appropriate, report as soon as possible to GU Ltd any deterioration, loss, theft or vandalism on these materials;

-          ensure the proper functioning of the Device used to complete the Transactions;

-          carry out the collection operations in accordance with the Partners' GTU: customers and Touch Point deposits, payment of invoices, purchases of goods and services, etc.;

-          carry out disbursement transactions in accordance with the Partners' GTU: customers and Touch Point withdrawal, transfer payments, etc.;

-          implement the commercial instructions given by GU Ltd;

-          comply with the Partners' GTU communicated by GU Ltd;

-          keep Transaction tickets and countersign Transactions reports provided by GU Ltd, in accordance with the instructions given during the GU Ltd training;

-          keep the documents in such a way that they will not be altered, stolen or destroyed;

-          buy the Device provided by GU Ltd according to the conditions defined in Annex 3 of this Agreement;

-          pay the monthly cost of connectivity as provided in article 4.1 of this Agreement.

 

 

Account Management.

The Touch Point is committed to:

 

-          maintain a minimum balance in the INTOUCH Account to facilitate loading and collection from customers;

-          be accountable to GU Ltd for Transactions from its INTOUCH Account and control access to such account through the protection of his password;

-          be responsible for his INTOUCH Account and assume all transactions carried out on his INTOUCH Account from the Device provided, for the duration of this Agreement.

 

Liquidity Management.

The Touch Point is committed to:

 

-          ensure a minimum available amount of liquidity for the completion of Transactions; this minimum amount is notified by GU Ltd on a case-by-case basis and based on the average of the daily Transactions performed by the Touch Point;

-          implement the liquidity management guidelines given by GU Ltd.

 

Compliance with Regulations in Force.

The Touch Point is committed to:

 

-          comply with applicable laws and regulations in the country where the Transactions take place;

-          indemnify GU Ltd against any losses, damages or expenses arising from the breach of any of its obligations set forth in this Agreement;

-          ensure proper identification of the customer when registering and presenting a valid ID document;

-          alert GU Ltd upon knowledge or suspicion that the account is being used for fraudulent or illegal purposes, or that the customer is in breach of the Partner's GTU provided for this purpose or is inappropriately using the service at the risk of compromising the integrity of the system.

 

Personal Responsibility.

The Touch Point is committed to:

 

-          refrain from any fraud, abuse or counterfeiting as part of his activities and his use of the One-Stop Software Package;

-          refrain from any improper or fraudulent use of customer identification data in the processing of Transactions performed via the One-Stop Software Package;

-          protect GU Ltd and the customer against any fraudulent subtraction of customer-supplied identification data, any modification or alteration of any information or identification data regarding a customer.


Any breach of the aforementioned provisions constitutes a breach of the contractual obligations of the Touch Point and exposes the Touch Point to penalties provided by the applicable laws and regulations.

 

Article 5. Duration

 

This Agreement is entered into for a period of twelve (12) months. It is renewable, after evaluation, by tacit agreement for successive periods of twelve (12) months.

 

Article 6 EXCLUSIVITY

 

This Agreement represents for the Parties a distribution agreement. There is no exclusivity clause in favor of the Touch Point.

 

Article 7 Commission

 

In return of each Transaction carried out, the Touch Point receives a commission under the terms and conditions defined by GU Ltd and/or the Partners as indicated in Annex 1.

 

 

Commissions are paid directly to the Touch Point by GU Ltd net of VAT.

 

No guarantee is given on the potential income that the Touch Point may earn from the performance of this Agreement. An extract from the commission plan indicating the rates in force is given to the Touch Point at the signature of the Agreement.

 

Commissions and profits are subject to change depending on the commercial policy of GU Ltd and/or the Partner. They will be cascaded to the Touch Point at least ten (10) calendar days before the introduction of the new rates. Any Touch Point not wishing to use these new rates shall notify GU Ltd at least five (05) calendar days before the implementation of the new pricing policy. In which case the Parties agree to meet to decide how to proceed with their collaboration under this Agreement. It is expressly approved by the Touch Point that the implementation of the new pricing policy whether it is accepted by the Touch Point shall not give rise to any compensation from GU Ltd and/or the Partner.

The absence of a response from the Touch Point implies agreement of this new pricing policy. This Agreement will be automatically amended and the new price list will form an integral part of the Agreement.

 

Once this Agreement has been signed by the Parties, an original copy is given to the Touch Point.

 

Article 8. CUSTOMER DATABASE MANAGEMENT

 

The Touch Point refrains from exploiting the customer information to which he would have access in the exercise of his obligations for purposes of commercial prospection without the prior written agreement of GU Ltd and the customer.

 

Article 9. Confidentiality

 

Throughout the duration of the Agreement, the Parties undertake to keep strictly confidential and not to disclose to any third parties by any means whatsoever the information received from either Party or to which they shall have access in the implementation of this Agreement.

 

To this end, the Parties undertake to take all necessary steps to keep and maintain these information confidential by their staff and/or subcontractors.

 

These confidentiality provisions under this Agreement shall apply for the duration of this Agreement and for at least five (5) years after its expiry or termination for any reason.

 

 

Article 10. Personal DATA

 

Each Party undertakes to comply with the legislation on protection of personal data under the performance of this Agreement.

 

To this end, each Party undertakes to not disclose to any third parties any personal data of the other Party, and not to use them for any other purpose than the performance of this Agreement.

 

In addition, each Party is required not to disclose to any third parties any customer Personal Data (personal and/or banking data) to which it may have access as part of this Agreement and the processing of Transactions. Failing that, the Party concerned shall be liable to the aggrieved Party, in particular for any loss suffered as a result of the disclosure or improper use of the Personal Data. The latter also reserves the right to terminate this Agreement and/or bring any judicial remedy authorized by the applicable laws and regulations.

 

The Parties agree to make available to customers any information necessary to enable them to exercise the rights conferred on them by the data protection and electronic transactions laws.

Article 11. Responsibility

 

The Touch Point is independent from GU Ltd. The latter cannot be held responsible for its fraudulent acts or omissions as part of a Service delivery by a Partner proposed by GU Ltd.

 

GU Ltd cannot be held responsible for any damage suffered by the Touch Point in connection with the use of a Partner's Service unless such damage resulted from a fault of GU Ltd.

 

GU Ltd is in no way responsible for the tortious acts committed by the Touch Point as part of his activities and Transactions performed on the One-Stop Software Package.

 

Article 12. FIGHT AGAINST MONEY LAUNDERING

 

GU Ltd is committed to communicate to the Touch Point all preventive procedures and measures, particularly in terms of customer identification and information escalation for the purpose of combating money laundering, the financing of terrorism and the proliferation of weapons.

 

Le Touch Point is committed to inform GU Ltd of any suspicious transaction carried out by his customers. GU Ltd will, if necessary, forward them to the Partner concerned who shall be responsible for declaring them to the competent authority.

 

Article 13. Intellectual PROPERTY

 

The Touch Point agrees that the provisions of this Agreement do not grant him any rights or title on any of the Partner Services and that he shall not attempt any action that would jeopardize these rights.

 

The Touch Point agrees that this Agreement does not grant him any right or title of any kind on the IT solution proposed by GU Ltd and that he shall not attempt any action that would jeopardize the rights of GU Ltd.

 

The Touch Point is committed not to use or communicate to any third parties the know-how and methodologies of GU Ltd or PARTNERS to which he may have access during the performance of the Agreement.

 

The provisions of this article shall survive the termination or expiry of this Agreement for a period of five (05) years.

Article 14. Force Majeure

 

In no case either Party could be liable when the facts that result in the responsibility are consecutive to a case of force majeure, i.e. to any unforeseeable, irresistible and insuperable event, or due to circumstances beyond the Parties control which make impossible the performance of their obligations. The occurrence of any case of force majeure or fortuitous event shall suspend the obligations of this Agreement within the limits of the degree to which the Party affected is precluded by the effects of the Force Majeure.

 

Each Party taking advantage of a case of force majeure must notify the other Party within a three days delay with a registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt.

 

As of the notification by one Party of the occurrence of a case of force majeure, the Parties seek together and in good faith the best solutions allowing to circumscribe it and to limit its consequences.

 

The existence of a case of force majeure during a period greater than fifteen (15) days can cause the termination of this Agreement without any compensation payable to either Party subject to prior notification.

 

This termination will take effect immediately after the dispatch of a registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt or a simple letter against a receipt notifying such decision.

 

Article 15. Termination

 

If one of the Parties fails to comply with its contractual obligations, the other Party may give notice by registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt.

 

The Agreement shall be automatically terminated without the need to resort to any additional formality if the other Party does not execute within the period mentioned in the formal notice.

Either Party may terminate this Agreement at the end of the twelve months period corresponding to the duration of this Agreement by giving a thirty days written notice to the other Party. In case of termination, the Touch Point is committed to:

 

-          stop providing the Partner Service via the Multimodal Platform and the One-Stop Software Package;

-          stop using the commercial name and brand of Partners or GU Ltd;

-          return to GU Ltd all promotional materials and information in its possession relating to Partners Services without any alteration;

-          return the equivalent of the INTOUCH Account balance to GU Ltd. Such funds will be subject to deductions from any loss suffered by GU Ltd.

 

Article 16. MISCELLANEOUS STIPULATIONS

 

Changes: Unless otherwise provided, the terms and conditions of this Agreement may only be changed by a written amendment signed by both Parties.

 

Partial Invalidity: If any provision of this Agreement becomes void under any law, regulation or court order, it shall be deemed unwritten. In this case, the Parties shall have to negotiate in good faith a replacement clause that is as close as possible to the nullity clause from a legal and economic point of view.

 

Independence of the Parties: The Parties declare and recognize that they are and shall stay independent professional partners throughout the duration of this Agreement, each party assuming the risks of its own activity.

 

Prohibition of Assignment: This Agreement is concluded intuitu personae, and shall not be assigned or transferred for any reason, by any person of any such, particularly in the form of transfer of operating assets, lease management of business capital or sales security or the contribution in the company without the express prior written consent of GU Ltd.

Article 17. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

 

Kenyan laws govern the validity, interpretation and performance of this Agreement. In the absence of an amicable settlement, the Nairobi courts have jurisdiction to settle any conflicts between the Parties.
